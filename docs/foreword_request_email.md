# Foreword request — e-mail template

A foreword written by an external authority strengthens both the international visibility of a Springer monograph and its standing for habilitation purposes. Below is a template e-mail for inviting a potential author. Replace the bracketed fields and adjust the tone to your relationship with the recipient.

---

**Subject:** Invitation to write a Foreword — Springer monograph on youth smart-city readiness

Dear Professor [Surname],

I hope this message finds you well. I am writing to invite you to contribute the **Foreword** to a forthcoming Springer Nature monograph that I am preparing with my co-author Prof. Dagmar Cagáňová (Newton University, Prague):

> **Digital Habits and Smart-City Readiness of Secondary-School Students: Bridging Mobile Ecosystems and Civic Digital Literacy**

The volume is the empirical and conceptual output of the **KEGA 004UCM-4/2023 project "Education for Smart Slovakia"**. It develops the **Youth Smart-City Readiness (YSCR)** composite indicator from four first-order indices — App Engagement, Digital Access Environment, School Digital Readiness, Curricular and Smart-City Awareness — and reports two original 2025 surveys of Slovak secondary-school students (Dataset A, N = 82; Dataset B, N = 401), supplemented by a public-data composite for 253 Slovak municipalities. Three external reviewers across three countries have already returned their assessments.

Your work on [specific topic — e.g. youth digital citizenship / smart-city governance / educational measurement] has been a reference point throughout, and a foreword from you would help readers situate our Slovak case in the broader European debate on youth digital citizenship and participatory smart cities.

I would be honoured if you would consider writing a short Foreword (typically **1–2 pages, ~600–900 words**) introducing the topic and the contribution of the volume.

Practical details:

- **Publisher:** Springer Nature / EAI Publishing

- **Foreword deadline:** [propose a date allowing 4–6 weeks lead time]

If you would prefer to read the manuscript first, I can send you a PDF of the full draft (approximately 230 pages) under embargo.

Thank you very much for considering this invitation.

## Practical points

- Send no earlier than four months and no later than six weeks before the manuscript submission date.
- Personalise the second paragraph — point to a concrete piece of the recipient's work that the monograph engages with.
- Attach the table of contents and abstract; do not attach the full manuscript on first contact.
- Once accepted, send a clean PDF and a one-page note summarising the four indices and the four personas, so the contributor has the substance at hand without re-reading the full text.
- Send personalised invitations in sequence (one or two at a time), not as a mass mailing — most senior scholars will only commit if they feel uniquely asked.
